# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People doing EMOM, Tabata, HIIT, or generic interval training — typically a phone
propped at the edge of a mat / pull-up bar / garage rack, hands sweaty, eyes on
the work. Glanceable in low attention windows of 1–2 seconds between reps.

## Product Purpose

Run interval workouts with sample-accurate audio cues so the user never has to
look at the screen mid-set. Save and reuse multiple workouts (EMOM and Intervals)
across devices once signed in. Install as a PWA, work offline.

## Brand Personality

Focused, athletic, precise. Stopwatch on a chain, not a cartoon. Honest about the
numbers. No motivational poster copy.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS cream/sand.** Warm off-white bg, tiny uppercase eyebrows above
  every section, neat icon-and-headline card grids.
- **Neon gradient "fitness app."** Magenta-purple-cyan gradients, glassmorphism,
  hero-metric cards, gradient text on the big timer.
- **Corporate dashboard sterility.** Gray-on-gray, dense data-table aesthetic,
  no personality, charts that aren't measuring anything.

## Design Principles

1. **Eyes on the bar.** The runner screen is the product. Everything else exists
   to get someone into a running timer quickly and out cleanly.
2. **Glanceable from across the room.** Big time. Loud bell. The phone is rarely
   in hand mid-workout; it's on the floor or a shelf.
3. **Sound is a first-class surface.** The bell, the ring-ring, and the tic-toc
   carry as much UX weight as anything visible.
4. **Honest about state.** Phase (WORK/REST), round number, and remaining time
   are always unambiguous. No guess-the-color.
5. **Guest-first, login-rewarded.** Anyone can run a workout in two taps; signing
   in unlocks saving and managing multiple.
